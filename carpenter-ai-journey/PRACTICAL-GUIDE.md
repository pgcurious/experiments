# Practical Guide: Following Ramesh's Path

## Companion to "The Carpenter's Question"

This guide translates Ramesh's journey into actionable steps you can take today.

---

## Week 1: Foundation

### Day 1-2: The List
**Action:** Write down every repetitive task in your day
- What questions do you answer repeatedly?
- What calculations do you do over and over?
- What takes time away from your actual craft?

**Tool needed:** Pen and paper (that's it)

---

### Day 3-4: Find Your Translator
**Action:** Identify someone who can help you
- A younger family member comfortable with technology
- A friend who uses smartphones confidently
- A local librarian (they're often great tech translators)

**What to ask them:** "Can you show me one simple tool that might help with [your smallest problem]?"

---

### Day 5-7: First Small Win
**Action:** Implement ONE simple automation

**Examples by profession:**
- **Carpenter/Handyman:** WhatsApp Business auto-responses
- **Chef/Caterer:** Google Forms for order requests
- **Tutor/Teacher:** Google Calendar for appointment scheduling
- **Farmer:** Weather alert apps with voice notifications
- **Tailor:** Photo gallery on phone to show previous work

**Success metric:** Did it save you even 15 minutes this week?

---

## Week 2-4: Voice Liberation

### Learn Voice-to-Text
**Why:** If typing is slow, voice removes the barrier

**How to start:**
1. On Android: Use Gboard (Google Keyboard)
   - Open any messaging app
   - Tap the microphone icon
   - Speak your message

2. On iPhone: Built-in dictation
   - Tap any text field
   - Tap the microphone icon on keyboard
   - Speak

**Practice:**
- Send voice-typed messages to family
- Create voice-typed notes about your day
- Write voice-typed emails

---

## Month 2: The Question Tool

### Getting Started with ChatGPT (or similar)

**Option 1: WhatsApp (Easiest)**
Some services offer ChatGPT through WhatsApp. Ask your translator to help you find current options.

**Option 2: Browser (Requires smartphone/tablet)**
- Go to chat.openai.com
- Create a free account (your translator can help)
- Ask your first question

**First Questions to Try:**
1. "How to write a professional email to a customer requesting payment?"
2. "[Your craft] tips for working in humid weather"
3. "What questions should I ask when hiring someone for [your profession]?"
4. "How to politely decline a customer request?"

**Rule:** Always verify important information. AI is helpful, not always perfect.

---

## Month 3: Documentation

### Create Your Knowledge Library

**What:** Record your expertise so others can learn

**How:**
1. **Video (Best):**
   - Use any smartphone
   - Record short clips (2-5 minutes) while you work
   - Explain what you're doing as you do it
   - Upload to private YouTube channel (translator can help set up)

2. **Voice Notes:**
   - Record tips and insights
   - Keep them in WhatsApp saved messages or Google Keep
   - Your future self (and employees) will thank you

3. **Photos with Notes:**
   - Take before/after photos of projects
   - Add voice-typed notes explaining decisions
   - Build a portfolio and a training library simultaneously

## The Carpenter's Principle

**Before adopting any tool, ask:**

1. **Does it serve my craft or distract from it?**
   - Serve: Yes → Continue
   - Distract: No → Discard

2. **Can I learn it in under 1 hour?**
   - Yes → Try it
   - No → Simplify or delay

3. **Will I use it at least 3 times per week?**
   - Yes → Worth learning
   - No → Skip for now

4. **Does it create more work than it saves?**
   - Saves time → Keep
   - Creates work → Remove

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### 1. Tool Hoarding
**Wrong:** "Let me learn 10 AI tools at once!"
**Right:** "Let me master this one tool first."

### 2. Perfectionism
**Wrong:** "I need to understand how AI works before using it."
**Right:** "I need to understand if this specific tool helps me."

### 3. Tech Person Syndrome
**Wrong:** "I should learn coding/programming."
**Right:** "I should learn to ask clear questions."

### 4. Craft Abandonment
**Wrong:** "AI can do the design work for me."
**Right:** "AI can handle the admin so I can focus on design."

## The Path to 10 People

Ramesh's formula (adaptable to any craft):

**At 3 people (you + 2):**
- Use AI for customer communication
- Use AI for scheduling
- Use AI for basic accounting

**At 5 people:**
- Add inventory/supply management tools
- Add training documentation (videos, notes)
- Add basic marketing automation

**At 7 people:**
- Add project management tools (who's doing what)
- Add payment/invoice automation
- Consider hiring an operations person (who's tech-comfortable)

**At 10 people:**
- You focus on craft + strategy
- Operations person handles tools + admin
- AI handles repetitive tasks
- Craftspeople focus on crafting

## Finding Your Mrs. Verma

**Where to look:**
- Local libraries (ask for digital literacy help)
- Community centers (often have tech help sessions)
- Adult education classes
- Younger family members (with patience)
- Retired teachers in your neighborhood

**What to ask for:**
"I don't want to become a tech expert. I just want to learn one or two simple tools that help my business. Can you help me, at my pace?"

**Red flags (wrong translator):**
- They use jargon you don't understand
- They're impatient with your questions
- They push tools you don't need
- They don't respect your craft

**Green flags (right translator):**
- They explain in simple terms
- They relate tech to things you know
- They start small and build gradually
- They celebrate your small wins

## Emergency Contacts

**When stuck:**
1. Your translator (first call)
2. The tool's help section (have translator show you)
3. YouTube search: "How to [your problem]"
4. Local library tech help

**When overwhelmed:**
1. Step back
2. Return to your craft for a day
3. Remember: Technology serves you, not vice versa
4. Start smaller than you were trying
